Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI)
Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) is the process of gathering, analyzing, and interpreting data related to potential or existing cyber threats. This information is used to understand the tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) of cyber adversaries, and to identify indicators of compromise (IOCs). The goal of CTI is to provide actionable insights that help organizations anticipate, prevent, and respond to cyber threats, thereby enhancing their overall security posture. CTI encompasses strategic, operational, and tactical intelligence to support decision-making at various levels within an organization.

It exists on overlay networks, such as Tor (The Onion Router), which anonymize users' identities and activities. The dark web is often associated with illegal activities, but it also serves as a platform for privacy-conscious individuals and whistleblowers.

  • Servers
  • IOT Devices
  • Printers and Scanners
  • Point-of-Sale Systems (Devices used for transactions in retail environments)
Incident Response
An Incident Response Plan (IRP) is a systematic approach designed to manage and mitigate the effects of cybersecurity incidents, such as data breaches, malware infections, or other security threats. The primary goal of an IRP is to handle incidents in a way that limits damage and reduces recovery time and costs.
Vulnerability Assessment
A vulnerability assessment is a systematic process used to identify, evaluate, and prioritize security weaknesses in an organization's IT infrastructure, applications, and processes. The goal is to identify vulnerabilities that could be exploited by threats and to recommend appropriate measures to mitigate the risks.
Penetration Test
A penetration test, often referred to as a "pen test," is a simulated cyber attack against an organization's IT infrastructure to identify and exploit vulnerabilities. The goal of a penetration test is to evaluate the security of the system by attempting to breach its defenses, thereby providing a real-world assessment of its resilience to cyber threats
