PII (PERSONALLY IDENTIFIABLE INFORMATION)

What is PII?
PII acronym for Personally Identifiable Information refers to any data that could potentially identify a specific individual.

1. Definition
PII includes information that can be used to distinguish or trace an individual's identity, either alone or when combined with other personal or identifying information. Common examples include:

  • Full Name
  • Social Security Number
  • Driver's License Number
  • Passport Number
  • Email Address
  • Phone Number
  • Physical Address
  • Biometric Data (Fingerprints, Facial Recognition data)


2. Types of PII
PII can be classified into two categories:
  • Direct Identifiers: Information that can directly identify an individual, such as name, Social Security number, or biometric data.
  • Indirect Identifiers: Information that, when combined with other data, can identify an individual, such as date of birth, gender, or ZIP code.


3. Legal and Regulatory Considerations
Various laws and regulations govern the collection, storage, and processing of PII to protect individuals' privacy and prevent data breaches. Some key regulations include:
  • GDPR - General Data Protection Regulation (more)
  • CCPS - California Consumer Privacy Act (more)
  • HIPAA - Health Insurance Probability and Accountability Act (more)
  • FISMA - Federal Information Security Management Act (more)
  • PCI DSS - Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (more)


4. Best practices for Handling PII
To protect PII, organizations should implement best practices such as:
  • Data Minimization: Collect only the necessary data required for a specific purpose
  • Access Control: Restrict access to PII to authorized personnel only.
  • Encryption: Use encryption to protect PII during transmission and storage.
  • Regular Audits: Conduct regular audits and assessments to ensure compliance with data protection policies.
  • Training: Educate employees on the importance of data privacy and security measures.
  • Incident Response: Develop and implement an incident response plan to address data breaches promptly.
